Biography

Kim Tae-ri, born on April 24, 1990, in Seoul, South Korea, is a gifted actress known for her captivating performances on the silver screen. Raised in a humble family, her love for acting blossomed during her school years, where she actively participated in drama clubs and local theater productions.

Despite challenges, Kim Tae-ri fearlessly pursued her acting dreams. Her breakthrough came with the lead role in Park Chan-wook's "The Handmaiden" (2016), earning widespread praise for her portrayal of "Sook-hee."

From charismatic swindlers to brave astronauts, Kim Tae-ri's versatility shone through in diverse roles, garnering international recognition and a loyal fan base.

Beyond her acting prowess, she serves as a role model, inspiring aspiring actors to pursue their passions. Kim Tae-ri continues to elevate Korean cinema, leaving an indelible mark on the global stage.

Her journey from drama enthusiast to acclaimed actress reflects her tenacity and dedication to her craft. As she lights up screens with her talent, the future holds endless possibilities for this rising star in the Korean entertainment industry.

Career Highlights

Twenty-Five Twenty-One (2022)

Kim Tae-ri won the Best Actress Award for Television at the 58th Baeksang Arts Awards for her portrayal of Na Hee-do, a fencing prodigy who dreams of competing in the Olympics.

Space Sweepers (2020)

Kim Tae-ri starred in the Korean sci-fi film Space Sweepers, which became the first Korean film to be released on Netflix. The film was a critical and commercial success, and Kim Tae-ri's performance was praised for its humor and emotional depth.

Little Forest (2018)

Kim Tae-ri starred in the film adaptation of the Japanese manga series Little Forest, which tells the story of a woman who returns to her hometown to reconnect with nature and herself. Kim Tae-ri's performance was praised for its naturalism and authenticity.

When the Day Comes (2017)

Kim Tae-ri made her film debut in the historical drama When the Day Comes, playing a young woman who witnesses a murder and is forced to go on the run. Her performance was praised for its strength and vulnerability, and it helped to launch her career.

The Handmaiden (2016)

Kim Tae-ri's breakout role was as Sook-hee in Park Chan-wook's critically acclaimed film The Handmaiden. Her portrayal of the spirited and cunning handmaiden garnered widespread acclaim, earning her recognition both locally and internationally. The film's success marked a turning point in her career, catapulting her into the spotlight as a talented and versatile actress.

Awards and Recognitions

Kim Tae-ri has received numerous awards and honors for her acting prowess, both domestically and internationally. Some of her most notable awards include:

Best Actress at the 58th Baeksang Arts Awards for her role in Twenty-Five Twenty-One (2022)

Best Actress at the 25th Chunsa Film Art Awards for her role in Little Forest (2018)

Best Actress at the 2017 Seoul International Film Festival for her role in The Handmaiden (2016)

Best New Actress at the 37th Blue Dragon Film Awards for her role in The Handmaiden (2016)

Best Actress at the 19th Busan Film Critics Awards for her role in The Handmaiden (2016)

Quotes on Her approach to acting

"I think acting is about being present in the moment and really feeling the emotions of the character. It's about being able to connect with the audience on an emotional level."

"I don't think there's any one right way to act. Every actor has their own unique process. For me, it's important to be able to lose myself in the character and really become them."

"I'm always learning and growing as an actor. I'm always looking for new ways to challenge myself and to grow as an artist."
